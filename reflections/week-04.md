1. This week I learned how different certificate formats like PEM, DER, and PFX work and how they relate to different operating systems. I also learned how trust stores work and how a certificate is validated through a chain back to a trusted root CA. One of the biggest things I learned is that a certificate can still fail even if it is valid, depending on the format or trust store.
   
2. The most challenging concept for me was understanding the difference between verifying a certificate and inspecting a certificate. At first, I thought certain outputs meant the certificate was trusted, but I learned that only specific commands actually confirm trust, while others just show information.
   
3. This concept appears in real-world systems when companies use their own root CA and need to push it to employee devices using tools like Group Policy or MDM. It also shows up when websites use certificates for HTTPS and when systems reject certificates due to format or trust issues.
