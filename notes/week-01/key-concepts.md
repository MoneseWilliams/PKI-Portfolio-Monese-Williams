# Week 01 — Key Concepts

## PKI Explanation (3–5 Sentences)

PKI is a system that proves a subject's identity through a certificate that is issued by the Certificate Authority. Certificates bind public keys to a subject's identity that can be shared. Private keys are paired with public keys that prove control of the subject's identity and must be kept protected. To establish trust, other systems must validate the certificate by working back up the trust chain to the Root CA who digitally signed the certificate 

Focus on:
- The problem PKI solves
- The role of keys
- The role of certificates
- How trust is established
